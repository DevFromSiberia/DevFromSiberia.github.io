Mstislav Vsevolodovich Keldysh

Soviet scientist in the field of mathematics and mechanics

He was one of the key figures behind Soviet space program. Among scientific circles of USSR Keldysh was known with epithet" the Chief Theoretician" in analogy with epithet "the Chief Designer" used for Sergey Korolyov.

Biography

  • 1911 - Keldysh was born in Riga
  • 1937 - Keldysh became Doctor of Science with his dissertation entitled Complex Variable and Harmonic Functions Representation by Polynomial Series
  • 1940 - Keldysh became the leader of a group of applied mathematicians involved in almost all large scientific projects of the Soviet Union.
  • 1954 - Keldysh, Sergey Korolyov and Mikhail Tikhonravov submitted a letter to the Soviet Government proposing development of an artificial satellite to orbit the Earth.
  • 1955 - Keldysh was appointed chairman of the Satellite Committee at the Academy of Science.
  • 1961 - Keldysh was elected President of the Academy of Sciences and kept this position for 14 years.
  • 1962 he was elected a member of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union.
  • 1978 - Keldysh was 67 when he died.
